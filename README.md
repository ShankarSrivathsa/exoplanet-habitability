# Habitable Earth-like (structurally) Exoplanets
This research has made use of the NASA Exoplanet Archive, which is operated by the California Institute of Technology, under contract with the National Aeronautics and Space Administration under the Exoplanet Exploration Program.

---
Kepler 452b is one of the most popular habitable exoplanets observed, but it is 60% larger than Earth. This piqued my interest and I started to find the "earth-like" exoplanet in terms of size and structre. 
This project mainly focuses on Terrestrial regime exoplanets and excludes Sub-Neptunes and Gas Giants regimes.
